Next-Generation RF Device Simulation

It all started with a request from a manufacturer I needed to identify which characteristics are essential in modern electronics design. I framed the task through the lens of RF simulation and, together with domain experts, refined the technical requirements and user scenarios.

Innovating and Improving

Our product already had a foundation for analyzing RF circuits, but understanding its capabilities was another challenge. I reviewed the existing simulation implementation and assessed its algorithmic complexity, which revealed significant limitations for analyses inputs. While learning the tool, I decided to investigate failures and untested areas recorded in our tracking system. Based on these findings, I outlined an MVP and began implementing the missing functionality.

To address algorithmic limitations, I developed a prototype of a new solver and evaluated its efficiency: profiling confirmed gains in runtime and memory usage. I further optimized solver parameters for real RF problems and handed over debug-mode development to the team for rapid iteration.

From MVP to an Engineer-Ready Product

The MVP proved its viability on test cases from the manufacturer. I proposed a joint testing plan and paved the way for further product development. Ultimately, we launched a full-scale industrial version the first of its kind.
